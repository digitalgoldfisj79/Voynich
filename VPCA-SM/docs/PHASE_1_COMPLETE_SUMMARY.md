### 2. DISTRIBUTIONAL PATTERNS ⚠️ (Exploratory Observations)

**Finding:** Multiple patterns show strong section-specific distributions

#### Pattern 2a: 'da-i-in' Procedural Concentration

**Observation:**
- Appears in 80.5% of procedural contexts (herbal + recipes)
- Strongly enriched in herbal (1.78x, p<10⁻³⁵)
- Depleted in zodiac (4.7% vs 9.6% expected)

**Confidence:**
- **Pattern exists:** 90% (effect size huge, statistically robust)
- **Semantic meaning (preparation):** 15-20% (exploratory hypothesis only)

**Status:** ⚠️ Strong candidate for Phase 2 validation

**Cannot claim yet:** "'da-i-in' means preparation" - needs hold-out testing

---

#### Pattern 2b: 'e' Medial Position Preference

**Observation:**
- Appears 91.8% in medial position (vs 74.7% expected, p<10⁻⁶⁵)
- Forms 40+ compound patterns
- Strong co-occurrence clustering (50 enriched tokens)

**Confidence:**
- **Pattern exists:** 95% (effect enormous, highly robust)
- **Semantic meaning (connector):** 20-25% (exploratory hypothesis)

**Status:** ⚠️ Excellent candidate for external validation (medieval corpus)

**Cannot claim yet:** "'e' is a connector" - needs independent validation

---

#### Pattern 2c: 'ol' Element Distribution

**Observation:**
- Modest enrichment in Earth zodiac signs (16.18% vs 12.11%, p<0.001)
- Multiple post-hoc hypothesis adjustments
- Fails strict Bonferroni correction (p>0.0005)

**Confidence:**
- **Pattern exists:** 60% (modest effect, p<0.001)
- **Semantic meaning (earth):** 10% (likely overfit)

**Status:** ⚠️⚠️ Weak exploratory finding, possibly spurious

**Deprioritize:** Needs independent evidence before further investigation

## LESSONS LEARNED

### Scientific Process:
1. **Separate exploration from confirmation** clearly from start
2. **Start with conservative estimates**, adjust up with evidence
3. **Build QC into process**, don't add after
4. **Recognize circular reasoning** immediately
5. **Be honest about limitations** upfront

### Confidence Assessment:
1. Exploratory findings: max 20-30% semantic confidence
2. Single test type: insufficient for >50%
3. Multiple independent validations needed for >60%
4. External validation critical

### What Good Looks Like:
- Clear about analysis type (exploratory vs confirmatory)
- Honest about data mining concerns
- Conservative confidence by default
- Validation designed before testing
- All results reported (including null)
