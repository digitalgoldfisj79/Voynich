### Conservative Interpretation:
**View:** Prior examination for any purpose = contaminated  
**Independent validations:** 1 (external corpus only)  
**Confidence:** 65-70%  
**Reasoning:** Safest approach, highest standard for "independent"

**Strengths:**
- Most defensible to skeptical reviewers
- Clear definition of "independent"
- No ambiguity

**Weaknesses:**
- Very strict standard (uncommon in practice)
- Discounts consistent manuscript-wide pattern
- Doesn't credit different hypothesis testing

### Liberal Interpretation:
**View:** Different hypotheses on same data = independent validations  
**Independent validations:** 6 (5 manuscript + 1 external)  
**Confidence:** 75-80%

**Strengths:**
- Consistent with standard scientific practice
- Acknowledges robustness of pattern

**Weaknesses:**

## ADDRESSING REVIEWER CONCERNS

### Potential Concern: "Not truly independent"
**Response:** 
"We acknowledge that manuscript sections were examined for morphology in Phase 1. However, positional grammar was a distinct, pre-registered hypothesis tested in Phase 2. This approach is consistent with standard corpus linguistic methodology, where multiple independent hypotheses are tested on the same corpus."

### Potential Concern: "Could be overfitting"
**Response:**
"To address overfitting concerns, we: (1) pre-registered all predictions with SHA documentation, (2) validated the pattern in a completely independent external corpus (Latin), which showed consistent results (88.5% medial), and (3) found the pattern across all sections with no exceptions."

### Potential Concern: "External validation only counts"
**Response:**
"While external validation is the gold standard, the consistent pattern across all manuscript sections (71.9-86.9% medial) demonstrates robustness. The external corpus validation (88.5% medial) supports the manuscript findings and suggests the pattern is not manuscript-specific."
