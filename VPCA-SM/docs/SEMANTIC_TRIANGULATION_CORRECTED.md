## üî¨ EPISTEMIC FRAMEWORK

### Confidence Levels Explained:

**Structural Confidence (What we measure):**
- Based on: VPCA clustering, distribution patterns, behavioral consistency
- High (70-75%): Strong evidence across multiple independent measures
- Medium (50-70%): Good evidence but some uncertainty
- Low (<50%): Weak or conflicting evidence

**Semantic Confidence (What we want but don't have):**
- Based on: Actual meaning assignments
- Current state: <20% for most roots
- Why so low: No independent semantic validation
- What's needed: Diagram tests, corpus matching, bilingual evidence

**Critical Rule:**
> **Never claim semantic confidence based only on structural evidence**
> 
> VPCA tells us word CLASSES, not word MEANINGS
> Like knowing "adjective" vs "verb" - category ‚â† definition

## üí° KEY LESSONS LEARNED

### 1. Distinguish Structure from Semantics
- VPCA gives categories, not contents
- Need independent validation for meanings
- Never claim semantic confidence based only on structural evidence

### 2. Medieval Parallels Are Suggestive, Not Proof
- Etymological connections are hypotheses
- Cherry-picking examples is not evidence
- Need systematic corpus comparison

### 3. Confidence Must Match Evidence
- High confidence requires multiple independent measures
- Speculation deserves low confidence
- Always separate "could be" from "probably is"

### 4. Overclaiming Hurts Science
- Better to admit uncertainty
- Design tests to reduce uncertainty
- Update beliefs based on evidence
